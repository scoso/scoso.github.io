Plan Details

In-Network Services

You can find out if a specific provider participates in the ElevateHealth network by checking the ElevateHealth provider directory:

  • On our online directory (updated weekly), select "Find a doctor." To find a PCP near your home or work, you may want to search by town.
  • For our printed ElevateHealth provider directory, ask your employer (if you have one), or call Harvard Pilgrim at (800) 848-9995.

IMPORTANT: You will need to ensure that authorization to see an Authorized Access provider is approved in advance in order to be covered for this care. These doctors, as well as Boston Children's Hospital, are indicated in the ElevateHealth provider directory (both online and in print) by the symbol ▲. Before seeing any new doctor or hospital, please check the ElevateHealth provider directory and make sure that providers are in the network, and at the location for your appointment. If the provider is not in the directory, or only at a different location, the care will not be covered under your plan.

Out-of-Network Services

Since you'll find nationally recognized hospitals in the ElevateHealth network, for both adults and children, it's unlikely you'll need care outside of the plan's providers. In the rare event that pediatric specialty care cannot be provided by an ElevateHealth provider, you have access to Boston Children's Hospital and some of its affiliated physicians.

Referrals and Authorizations

Specialty care is available with a referral from your PCP to an Easy Access specialist. Referrals are not necessary for some services, such as routine eye exams and most gynecological care. One of the advantages of ElevateHealth is that participating providers work closely together to ensure highly coordinated care. To be sure that sure you're covered for specialty care, you'll want to ask your PCP to refer you to a participating ElevateHealth provider every time you need specialty care.

In the rare instance you and your provider feel care is needed at Boston Children's Hospital or one of its affiliated providers, your PCP or specialist will submit a request for authorization to Harvard Pilgrim. Your provider will need to provide information about the member's condition and why it can't be treated by an in-network (Easy Access) provider. Harvard Pilgrim will review the request and respond back to you within two working days. This time frame may be extended if additional information is needed from your provider.

Once a decision is made, you will receive a letter from Harvard Pilgrim stating that your request has either been approved or denied, and a copy of the letter will be sent to both your provider (who requested the authorization) and the specialist from whom care was requested. You can also track the status of your request through HPHConnect.

NOTE: If your request is approved, the letter will detail what care has been authorized, and for how long the authorization is valid. If the authorization is denied: Your letter will include information listing alternative in-network (Easy Access) providers who can give you the care you need. If a member sees an Authorized Access provider without prior authorization: Your insurance will not cover the visit and you'll be responsible for paying the full cost of this care.

Medical Emergencies

Harvard Pilgrim covers all medical emergencies, including heart attack, stroke, choking, loss of consciousness or seizures. Just go to the nearest emergency facility or call 911 or another local emergency number. With the ElevateHealth HSA HMO, emergency room care services are subject to the deductible. Please see the Summary of Benefits for details.

If you are hospitalized with a medical emergency, you must call Harvard Pilgrim within 48 hours, or as soon as you can (or ask someone to do this for you). If your attending physician notifies your PCP, they will call Harvard Pilgrim and arrange for any follow-up care you may need.

Additional Coverage

When you're traveling, you're covered for care you may need if you become sick or injured. Harvard Pilgrim covers unexpected or unforeseen care, such as for earaches, flu, etc. when you're traveling outside the state in which you live.

A large number of preventive tests and services are also covered at no charge. Please note that you may receive some tests and services that are subject to the deductible during a preventive visit.
